How to Generate Subtitles and Add Them to a Video

By AudioTools Editorial Team | Published April 30, 2026

What Are Subtitles?

Subtitles are timed lines of text that appear alongside a video so viewers can follow spoken dialogue or narration. They are useful for accessibility, silent viewing, translation workflows, and content repurposing.

How to Generate Subtitles

Upload your audio or video file to an online subtitle generator, let the tool create a transcript, then review the wording and timing before export. This is the core workflow behind most automatic subtitle generator tools.

How to Add Subtitles to a Video

After you generate subtitles, export the result as an SRT or VTT file. Then import that subtitle file into your video editor, publishing platform, or player that supports captions or subtitles.

Can I Export Transcripts as SRT or VTT?

Yes. SRT and VTT are two of the most common subtitle and caption formats. SRT is widely supported across editors and video platforms, while VTT is especially common in web-based playback environments.

Can I Translate Subtitles into Multiple Languages?

Yes, if your workflow supports translation, you can use the transcript as the base for multilingual subtitle preparation or translate transcripts to English before final review. That is one reason subtitle editors and caption generator software often start with a transcript first.
