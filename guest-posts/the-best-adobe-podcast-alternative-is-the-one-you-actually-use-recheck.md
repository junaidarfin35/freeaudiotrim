# Recheck pass for "The best Adobe Podcast alternative is the one you actually use"

## Goal

Make sure the guest post reads like a fresh editorial article, not a spun SEO page and not a typical AI draft.

## AI-pattern risks found in earlier drafts

- Too many sentences followed the same "If you need X, use Y" rhythm.
- The recommendation sections felt tidy in a machine-like way.
- Some lines sounded like product positioning instead of a person making a call.
- The ending was too polished and generic.

## Fixes applied in the final version

- Switched the article angle from "best alternatives list" to "pick the tool that matches the size of the job."
- Broke up the cadence with shorter punches, longer reflections, and less symmetrical paragraph structure.
- Reduced formulaic comparison language and kept the competitor mentions inside a more natural editorial flow.
- Kept FreeAudioTrim as the first recommendation, but made the case through workflow, privacy, and speed instead of sales-heavy claims.
- Removed em dashes and avoided obvious AI phrases like "let's dive in," "at its core," "game changer," and "the future looks bright."
- Kept headings in sentence case and used direct language instead of inflated "AI copy" vocabulary.

## Manual verdict

The final draft reads like a human opinion piece aimed at creators who are tired of overbuilt workflows. It still has structure, but it no longer reads like a template, a spinner, or a soft sales page pretending to be an article.
